Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who revolutionized science with the theory of relativity (\(E=mc^{2}\)) and the photoelectric effect (1921 Nobel Prize). Known for his profound contributions to quantum theory and cosmology, he worked in a patent office before becoming a renowned professor in Europe and the US. He was a passionate pacifist and humanitarian who emigrated to the US in 1933. 

We can say without any doubt that Albert Einstein was one of the greatest scientists in history. He was curious, imaginative, and deeply intelligent. From a young age, he showed interest in mathematics and physics. His ideas changed the way we understand space, time, energy, and the universe itself.

Einstein worked as a patent clerk before becoming a famous scientist. Despite facing many challenges, he continued to question traditional ideas and developed revolutionary theories. His work still influences modern science and technology today.

Key Information and Legacy:

  • Born on 14 March 1879 in Ulm, Germany
  • Famous for the Theory of Relativity
  • Gave the equation E = mc²
  • Won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921
  • Worked on Quantum Theory and Statistical Mechanics
  • Moved to the United States in 1933
  • Became a U.S. citizen in 1940
  • Died on 18 April 1955 in the USA
